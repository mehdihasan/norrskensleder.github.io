# Learning Sailing in Malmö – My Daily Diary

I took a sailing course at Malmö Segelsällskap (https://www.malmosegelsallskap.se/).

This is my daily diary of the experience.

## About the Location

Malmö Segelsällskap is located near the coast of Malmö, a beautiful city in southern Sweden. The sailing area is calm, open, and perfect for beginners. The harbor is well organized, and the surrounding area is peaceful, with a great view of the sea.

## About the Öresund Strait

The Öresund Strait lies between Sweden and Denmark. It connects the Baltic Sea to the North Sea. The strait is around 118 km long and varies in depth, but it is generally not very deep compared to open oceans.

One of its most famous landmarks is the Öresund Bridge, which connects Malmö (Sweden) to Copenhagen (Denmark). It is a combined road and rail bridge.

The wind conditions here are usually moderate, which makes it a good place for sailing training. Waves are not too large, but they can change depending on the wind. There are also currents, but they are manageable for beginners.

## Why I Became Interested in Sailing

I have always been an explorer at heart. In my childhood, I used to read stories about explorers and adventurers from around the world. But I was born and raised in a lower-middle-class, urban family in Bangladesh, so my opportunities were limited.

Still, I kept pushing myself to learn whatever I could. I learned swimming in a river. I practiced using paddle boats and kayaks in ponds and canals. I even tried small boats in the sea.

I learned how to ride a bike after getting married. I traveled to remote places in Bangladesh with very little preparation.

After moving to Sweden, a new world opened up for me. I learned how to drive and went on road trips across different European countries. I also got a drone operation license.

Now I am learning sailing. Maybe one day I will sail to the Cape of Good Hope-who knows!

In Sweden, there is a benefit called “friskvårdsbidrag.” It means your employer gives you money for health and sports activities. I found out that sailing is included as a sport. As soon as I learned that, I decided to use this benefit to take a sailing course.

### 2026-06-30

The weather was sunny and warmer than yesterday. We started to build a good connection with each other.

This time, we prepared the boat ourselves. That included unwrapping the sails, setting up the mainsail, and attaching the jib.

We sailed near the Öresund Bridge and spent around three hours on the water. Each of us practiced both tacking and gybing several times.

I felt more confident today. However, I still find the direction terms like “close-hauled” a bit confusing. Apart from that, I think I did well.

We also took photos of each other and enjoyed the sunny weather. By the end of the session, I felt quite tired.

### 2026-07-01

The weather was nice today. It was a mix of sun and clouds, and the sun was not too strong. It felt more pleasant than yesterday.

Today we had a plan to race against a junior team. Before going out, we had a short briefing about the basic rules of sailing races.

Our second instructor was not with us today. So it was only our main instructor and the students. This meant we had to prepare the boat by ourselves, which was a good learning experience.

We took the boat out of the harbor and started our first race within about 20 minutes. It was very exciting. The tacking and gybing felt intense, and a few times we almost slipped on the deck.

The most thrilling part was trying to block the other boat. To push them away from the course, we tried to gybe around them and come close from behind. It felt a bit scary, but it was all part of the race.

We won two races and had to abandon one.

Each of us took turns handling different roles-steering, managing the jib, and controlling the mainsail. There were no accidents, except for one accidental gybe, but no one was hurt. Our team bond became stronger today.

The most important lesson for me today was this: sailing is all about understanding the wind and the current. If you can read them well, sailing becomes much easier.
